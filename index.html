The Framework

There's a machine underneath every major crisis. Most people never see it.

Game theory was built to model nuclear deterrence. Today the same logic governs oil blockades, currency wars, and supply chain collapse — running simultaneously, feeding each other.

Nash Equilibrium

Crises don't end — they lock.

No player can improve their situation by changing strategy alone. The outcome nobody wanted becomes the only stable state.

Prisoner's Dilemma

Both defect, both lose.

The structure of sanctions, blockades, and arms races. Defection isn't irrational — it's the only rational move given what everyone else is doing.

Fear Multiplier (λ)

Once it crosses a threshold, cooperation breaks.

Every aggressive move raises systemic tension for all players — including neutral ones. The math changes. Defection becomes dominant.

Cascade Dynamics

One shock triggers the next.

Energy → fertilizer → food → migration. Each domain is linked. The simulation models these chains in real time so you can see what's coming.
